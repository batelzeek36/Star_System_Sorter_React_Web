1. Read `SHORT_FORM_CONTENT.md` (all of it - it's your discovery engine)
2. Pick 3 scripts from "Gate Lore Drops" or "Research Flex"
3. Record them on your phone (30-60 seconds each)

1. Read `SOFT_REVEAL_GAMEPLAN.md` (sections: "What You CAN Show", "What You CANNOT Show", "The Pitch")

7. Go live and have fun

### If You're Planning Long-Term:

1. Read all three documents in order
2. Customize the timeline in `SOFT_REVEAL_GAMEPLAN.md` to fit your schedule
3. Set up your Discord server (channels, roles, welcome message)
4. Create your Google Form for beta tester applications

1. Read `COLLABORATION_FRAMEWORK.md` (section: "Collaboration Tiers")

5. Start small (beta tester or small contribution) and scale up if they prove themselves

## Key Principles (Remember These)

### 0. Consistency is Credibility

**Always say "1,200+ hours"** - not "862-1,656 hours" or any range. Pick one number and repeat it everywhere. Ranges invite nitpicks and sound uncertain. You did the work. Own the number.

### 1. Show the Magic, Hide the Method

People don't need to see the mappings to be impressed by the results. Show them the experience, not the engine.

### 2. Protect the Moat

Your competitive advantage is the 1,200 hours of research. The mappings and prompts are the crown jewels. Never reveal them publicly.

### 3. Build in Public (Carefully)

Share your process, your struggles, your wins. But always stay on the safe side of the IP line.

### 4. Attract, Don't Convince

You're not trying to convert skeptics. You're trying to find the people who already get it. Speak to them.

### 5. Collaborate, Don't Compromise

You need help, but you don't need to give away equity or control to get it. Start small, scale up with proven people.

### 6. Stay in Your Voice

You're a researcher-builder, not a guru. You're curious, rigorous, collaborative, passionate, and humble. Stay there.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Revealing Too Much Too Soon

Don't show the mappings or prompts, even if people beg. That's the moat.

### ‚ùå Overpromising Results

Don't claim this will "change your life" or "predict your future." Frame it as a pattern recognition tool for self-discovery.

### ‚ùå Giving Equity Too Easily

Don't give equity to people you haven't worked with. Start with profit-sharing, scale to equity if they prove themselves.

### ‚ùå Ignoring Legal Protection

Don't skip the contracts. Even with friends. Especially with friends.

### ‚ùå Trying to Convince Skeptics

Don't waste energy on people who don't get it. Focus on the people who do.

### ‚ùå Burning Out

Don't stream every day or respond to every DM. Pace yourself. This is a marathon, not a sprint.

### ‚ùå Losing Your Voice

Don't try to sound like a guru or a corporate brand. Stay authentic. You're a researcher-builder who got obsessed with a question.

## When to Pivot

### If No One Cares:

- Reassess your messaging (are you leading with the right hook?)
- Try a different platform (maybe your audience is on TikTok, not Twitter)
- Show more results, less process (people want to see the magic, not the method)

### If People Want to Copy You:

- That's validation. It means you're onto something.
- Protect your IP (don't reveal the mappings or prompts)
- Move faster (launch publicly before they can catch up)

### If You're Getting Overwhelmed:

- Narrow your focus (pick one platform, one stream per week)
- Delegate (find a community manager, a video editor, a social media scheduler)
- Say no more (you don't have to respond to every inquiry)

### If You're Not Having Fun:

- Remember why you started (you're obsessed with this question, not with being an influencer)
- Take a break (it's okay to go dark for a week)
- Reconnect with the research (the work is the reward, not the attention)
